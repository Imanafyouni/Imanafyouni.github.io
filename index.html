About

Energy storage, from atoms to cells

I am a materials scientist and mechanical engineer specializing in energy storage materials and materials characterization. I hold an M.Sc. in Materials Science from Pittsburg State University, where my research focused on developing and optimizing manganese and vanadium based active materials for zinc-ion and lithium-ion batteries, evaluating carbon coating and functionalization strategies, and characterizing structural evolution using XRD and SEM/EDS.

Before graduate school I optimized supercapacitor performance through electrolyte development at the University of Houston, and I spent four years in manufacturing R&D and quality assurance, coordinating cross-functional teams across R&D, engineering, and production to deliver quality products on time.

I bring practical expertise in material synthesis, electrochemical testing, and advanced characterization, with a track record of improving material performance through data-driven experimentation and disciplined process optimization. I am seeking process engineering and R&D roles in the energy, advanced manufacturing, and specialty materials industries.

Research Projects

Battery cathode research

Graduate research on next-generation cathode materials for zinc-ion batteries, a safer and more sustainable alternative to lithium-ion chemistry. Presented as poster presentations at national and university research venues.

ACS Fall 2025 · Poster presentation

Electrochemical Performance of Nickel Vanadate as the Cathode in Zinc Batteries

Investigated a Ni₂V₂O₇/V₂O₅/NiO composite (nickel vanadate, NVO) as a cathode for zinc-ion batteries. Characterized structure and morphology by XRD and SEM, and evaluated electrochemical behavior by cyclic voltammetry, galvanostatic charge-discharge, and impedance spectroscopy. Structural stabilization and enhanced ion transport drove a strong capacity increase over extended cycling.

PSU Research Colloquium 2025 · Poster presentation

Mn₃O₄ Nanostructures as Cathodes for High-Energy-Density Zinc-Ion Batteries

Synthesized pristine Mn₃O₄ nanostructures by a microwave-assisted solvothermal method and evaluated them as a cathode for zinc-ion batteries. Cyclic voltammetry showed enhanced redox kinetics and strong charge-storage capability, with phase and morphology confirmed by XRD and SEM. The material delivered high reversible capacity with excellent long-term cycling stability.
